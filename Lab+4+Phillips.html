def query_url(key,fieldnames,filterstring,sortvar,numpages):
    """
    This takes all of the necessary parameters to form a query 
    to college scorecard
    Input: key (data.gov API key, string), fieldnames (list of variable 
      names), filterstring (string for filtering), sortvar (variable to 
      sort with by descending), numpages (number of pages to return)
    Output: final request
    """
    baseurl = 'https://api.data.gov/ed/collegescorecard/v1/schools.json'
    finalurl = requests.get(baseurl,params={filterstring.split('=',1)[0]: filterstring.split('=',1)[1],
                                            '_fields':','.join(fieldnames),
                                            '_page' : numpages,
                                            '_per_page' : 100,
                                            '_sort' : sortvar,
                                            'api_key':key})
    return (finalurl)

Analysis¶

I first looked at the comparison between the debt that students who completed university vs Household Income. This showed that there was a somewhat positive linear relation between debt and income with poorer students having less debt. This was also present in the noncompleting student though most of the non completing students had lower debt compared to completing students for obvious reasons

I also looked at the poverty rate vs in state tuition and found that there was a few schools that had high poverty rate and had the lowest in state tuition but most were around the same poverty rate level when it came to in state tuition and it did not explain much. I then compared the poverty rate to the pell grant rate and found there was actually not much of a difference in poverty rate vs the Pell Grant rate. I chalked this up to the fact that the poverty rate was about the same for most of the schools so there could not be much deviation.

Looking at Pell Grant rate still, I looked at In State Tuition against Pell Grant rates and found that there seemed to be a negative correlation between the two which makes sense that poorer students would go to schools that are less expensive and that poorer students get Pell Grants (a need based grant). This was also shown with the In State Tuition vs Income which was positive, saying that more well off students went to more expensive schools.

The one thing I found most surprising was the positive relation between loan rate and pell grant rate. I assumed that the pell grants would help decrease the amount of inividuals that would require loans but it was a VERY strong positive correlation between the two. Obviously this is not to say this is causation, but it shows that even for those receiving Pell Grants, it is obviously not enough. I also looked at income vs Loan Rate and found it very flat, with there not being any real correlation whatsoever. I also found this interesting since I was going to assume it would mean those with low income might have to receive more loans but it just seems that everyone is required to take loans no matter what.
